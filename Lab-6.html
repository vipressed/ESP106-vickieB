Introduction

In this lab we will use some GPS tracking data for Blue Whales in the eastern North Pacific, downloaded from Movebank https://www.movebank.org which has lots of wildlife tracking data. We will make some maps and overlay polygons of Marine Protected Areas off the California coast to understand how whales move into and out of MPAs.

Protected areas

  1. The folder MPAs has a shapefile with polygons for Marine Protected Areas within the United States. This file was extracted from a file with all protected areas in the US source. Read the MPA shapefile.
mpa <- vect("mpas.shp")
  1. Now we will focus on just the US west coast. You can use the following longitude and latitude extent: xmin=-128, xmax=-115, ymin=32, ymax=40. Get just the MPAs on the US west coast by cropping the MPA dataset to this extent.
#Hint: use the crop() function
west_ext <- ext(-128,-115,32,40)
